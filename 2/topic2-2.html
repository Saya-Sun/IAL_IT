🌐 Network Transmission Media

Devices can be connected using wired or wireless connections.

💡 Exam Tip: Always compare wired vs wireless in exams
🔌 Wired Media
1

🔗 Twisted Pair Cable

Electrical signals • Cheap • Short distance
  • Up to 10 Gbps
  • Easy to install
  • Can suffer interference
  • Used in LAN & telephone
2

📡 Coaxial Cable

Radio signals • Less interference
  • Up to 10 Mbps
  • More protected than twisted pair
  • Hard to install
  • Used in TV & internet
3

💡 Fibre Optic Cable

Light signals • Very fast • No interference
  • Up to 1 Gbps
  • Long distance
  • Expensive & fragile
4

🔌 Powerline

Uses electrical wiring
  • Up to 2 Gbps
  • Very easy to install
  • Depends on wiring quality
📡 Wireless Media
1

📶 Microwaves

Line of sight • Point-to-point
  • High frequency
  • Used in towers
2

📡 Radio Waves

No line of sight • Travels through walls
  • Used in Wi-Fi
  • Short & long distance
3

🔴 Infrared

Short distance • Weak signal
  • Needs line of sight
  • Used in peripherals
4

🛰️ Satellite

Global communication
  • Uses radio waves
  • Used in GPS
  • Uses triangulation

⚠️ Exam Focus

Know the differences between transmission media types (speed, cost, interference, distance).

💡 Twisted Pair = cheap but interference
💡 Fibre Optic = fast + no interference (BEST)
