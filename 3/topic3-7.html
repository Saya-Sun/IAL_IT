🔐 Sharing Personal Data Online

📌 What is Personal Data?

Personal data is any information that can identify a person, such as name, address, email, location or login details.

This data is often required when creating accounts or using online services.

💡 Exam Tip: Personal data = identifiable information
⚠️ Security Risks
1

👁️ Data Exposure

Public access to personal data

If privacy settings are weak, third parties can collect information and build a profile of a user.

Impact: Identity theft and fraud

2

💻 Hacking

Unauthorised access

Hackers may break into systems and steal user data or take control of accounts.

Impact: Financial loss or criminal activity

3

📉 Data Breach

Poor security by organisations

If companies fail to protect data, large amounts of personal data can be leaked.

Impact: Loss of privacy for many users

4

🎣 Phishing

Fake messages to steal data

Users are tricked into entering personal details into fake websites or emails.

Example: Fake bank email asking for login details

🛡️ Protection Methods
1

🔑 Strong Passwords

Hard to guess

Use long, complex passwords with symbols and mixed characters.

Benefit: Reduces risk of hacking

2

🧬 Biometrics

Unique biological data

Fingerprint or face recognition is harder to copy or guess.

3

🔐 Encryption

Scrambles data

Data is converted into unreadable form and can only be accessed with a key.

Example: HTTPS, VPN

4

🔥 Firewall

Filters network traffic

Blocks malicious access and protects devices.

5

🔍 Check Emails & URLs

Avoid scams

Verify sender and links before entering personal data.

6

🔒 Privacy Settings

Control who sees your data

Limit visibility and be careful about what you share online.

⚖️ Evaluation

  • ✔ Online services require personal data
  • ✔ Useful for accounts and services
  • ❗ Risk of misuse and cybercrime
  • ❗ Strong security measures are essential

🧠 Quick Memory

  • Risks → hacking, phishing, breach
  • Protection → password, encryption, firewall
  • Always verify links & control privacy
