👣 Digital Footprint

📌 What is a Digital Footprint?

A digital footprint is a record of all the activities you carry out online. It shows where you have been and what you have done on the internet.

💡 Exam Tip: Digital footprint = record of online activity

🌐 Examples of Digital Footprint

  • Websites you visit
  • Emails and messages
  • Social media posts and likes
  • Files downloaded
  • Data entered online
🔍 Types of Digital Footprint
1

✍️ Active Digital Footprint

Data you share intentionally

Information you deliberately post or send online.

Examples: social media posts, emails, online purchases

2

🕵️ Passive Digital Footprint

Data collected without your knowledge

Information collected automatically by websites.

Examples: location tracking, website visits, cookies

⚠️ Why It Matters
1

📌 Permanent Record

Difficult to remove

Once data is online, it can be stored and reused.

2

💼 Reputation

Affects future opportunities

Employers may check your online activity.

3

🛑 Security Risks

Used for identity theft

Criminals can use your data for fraud.

⚖️ Active vs Passive


1

✍️ Active Digital Footprint

Data you choose to share
✅ Advantages
  • Allows self-expression (posts, opinions)
  • Builds online identity or personal brand
  • Helps communication and social interaction
⚠️ Disadvantages
  • Permanent record of actions
  • May damage reputation if inappropriate
  • Can expose personal information to others
2

🕵️ Passive Digital Footprint

Data collected without your knowledge
✅ Advantages
  • Improves user experience (personalised content)
  • Faster browsing (saved preferences, cookies)
  • Helps websites improve services
⚠️ Disadvantages
  • Loss of privacy
  • Data can be tracked and monitored
  • Risk of misuse by companies or hackers

🧠 Quick Memory

  • Digital footprint → online record
  • Active → you share
  • Passive → collected
  • Important → reputation + security
