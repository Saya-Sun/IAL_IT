🧩 Decomposition of Systems

📌 What is Decomposition?

Decomposition is the process of breaking a large IT system into smaller parts called sub-systems.

Why Decompose?

Makes systems easier to manage
  • Simplifies complex systems
  • Makes design and development easier
  • Helps identify errors quickly
  • Allows testing of each part separately
💡 Exam Tip: Decomposition = breaking system into smaller parts

Sub-systems

Smaller parts of a system

Each sub-system performs a specific task and works together to complete the whole system.

✈️ Example: Airport Check-in System

1

⚖️ Weighing Luggage

Measure luggage weight
  • Send data to system
  • Check weight limits
2

🎫 Scanning Boarding Pass

Scan barcode
  • Retrieve passenger data
  • Confirm check-in
3

🏷️ Printing Luggage Label

Generates and prints baggage tag
  • Includes passenger and flight details
  • Printer prints the label
  • Label is attached to luggage
💡 Exam Tip: Sub-system = input → process → output

🧠 Summary

Decomposition helps break large systems into manageable sub-systems, making them easier to design, test, and understand.

💡 Exam Tip: System → Sub-system → Task
