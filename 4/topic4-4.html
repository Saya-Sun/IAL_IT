Flowcharts

📌 What is a Flowchart?

A flowchart is a diagram that shows the sequence of steps and decisions in a process.

What it shows

  • Inputs and outputs
  • Processes (actions)
  • Decisions
  • Sequence of steps
💡 Exam Tip: Flowchart = steps + decisions (NOT data flow)

🔣 Flowchart Symbols

1

🔵 Terminator (Start/End)

Shows where the process begins and ends.

2

📥 Input / Output

Represents data entering or leaving the system.

3

⚙️ Process

Represents an action or operation.

4

🔷 Decision

Represents a choice (yes/no).

5

📦 Subprocess

A smaller process within a larger system.

6

➡️ Flow Line

Shows the direction of steps.

🎵 Example: Music Quiz System

i

📥 Inputs

  • Number of students
  • Student name
  • Student score
ii

⚙️ Processes

  • Store data
  • Count students
  • Assign certificate
iii

🔷 Decisions

  • Score ≥ 75 → Gold
  • Score ≥ 65 → Silver
  • Score ≥ 50 → Bronze
  • Else → Participation

📦 Subprocess

Used to simplify flowcharts

Complex parts (e.g. certificate decision) can be moved into a subprocess to make the main flowchart easier to read.

🧠 Activity

Design a flowchart for a shop loyalty system to calculate discounts based on spending and card type.

🧠 Summary

Flowcharts show the sequence of steps and decisions in a system, making processes easier to understand and design.

💡 Exam Tip: Flowchart = sequence of steps, DFD = movement of data
